What is the Counter?

A pop-up workspace inside your office.

We set up in your office for a few days. Individuals show up with a real problem they face in their work. Through the process, they learn to think and work with AI to diagnose their problem, build a working solution in hours, and leave not just with something they made themselves — but with the fluency and belief to keep working this way.

The process

How a Counter engagement works.

From finding the right starting point to building capability that sticks.

Step 1

We find the right starting point.

Before we arrive, we help you identify the people and problems that are the best fit for this experience. Specifically, those who have something they're working on right now that they wish was faster, better, or simply possible. It could be something for their team, their customers, or just themselves.

Step 2

Your people come to The Counter and build.

In pre-scheduled sessions, individuals sit down with us and their problem. Working side by side, we help them develop AI fluency in real time — learning how to think with AI to diagnose what's really going on, explore a wide range of potential solutions, and build a working minimum viable product that they can take back and test immediately.

Step 3

They take it back into the real work.

Between sessions, each person puts what they built to work — testing it against real data, real deadlines, and real colleagues. This is where the gap between a demo and a tool becomes obvious, and where the most valuable feedback comes from.

Step 4

We iterate and sharpen together.

They come back with what worked, what didn't, and what they wish it could do next. We rebuild, extend, and refine — not just the tool, but how they think about solving problems with AI. Each cycle compounds their fluency.

Step 5

You leave with evidence, not a pitch deck.

Working tools your people built themselves. Increased fluency they can apply again tomorrow. And the proof — visible to leadership, peers, and the builders themselves — that this way of working is real and worth investing in further.

01 — Filter, Don't Dig

Every metric recalculates the moment focus narrows.

She used to maintain separate spreadsheet tabs for each dispute type, each merchant category. One change meant updating across five sheets. Now she taps a filter pill and the entire view reshapes — KPIs recalculate, trends update, charts reflow. The focus that took hours to construct now takes seconds.

02 — Cross-Reference, Don't Pivot

The two-hour pivot table, replaced by two clicks.

Finding where disputes concentrate used to mean exporting to Excel, building a pivot table, formatting it for readability. Now she picks any two dimensions — dispute type and merchant category, volume and resolution time — and the intersection appears. No export. No rebuilding. The patterns she knew were there are suddenly visible.

03 — See What Changed

The 'what happened this week' view, now instant.

Every Monday morning, she needed to know what moved. Volume changes, exposure shifts, resolution speed deltas. She'd compile this manually — last week's numbers beside this week's, calculating percentage changes, sorting by magnitude. Now the WoW Delta view auto-sorts the deltas for her. The patterns that changed most rise to the top. Decision-ready in minutes.

04 — Generate the Report

The six-hour report, assembled in minutes.

Her weekly report to leadership isn't just dashboards — it's intelligence. A narrative summary of patterns and changes, conditional alerts for unusual activity, progress on key metrics. This used to mean pulling data, writing it in Word, formatting, revising. Now the Weekly Report tab generates a structured document with conditional narratives and threshold alerts built in.

The Shift

Same analyst. Same data. Completely different Monday.

The tool didn't make her smarter — she already knew the patterns. It removed the hours of assembly standing between her expertise and her team's inbox. The bottleneck was never analysis capacity. It was assembly time.
