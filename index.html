In Practice

She didn't get faster. She got back to the work she actually loves.

A food formulation scientist at a global ingredients company — decades of expertise, all stored in her head. Every project started from scratch, every week spent getting to a result she already knew was coming.

We introduced AI tools that let her simulate ingredient interactions digitally before going to the lab. A week's work was 90% done in a day. But what happened next is the whole point.

"I got to spend six days tinkering with things I'd love to do in school but never could here — carbonation, mouthfeel, flavor profiles. Things I never had time for."

That tinkering unlocked an entirely new service line — texture elevation — that the company had never been able to offer. The AI didn't replace the scientist. It gave her back the craft she went to school for. And in doing so, it created value the company didn't even know was possible.

Hackathons and boot camps don't change how anyone works. They never have.

You can teach a room full of people how to prompt. You can run a two-day innovation sprint. You can hand out tool licenses. And at the end of it, almost everyone goes back to doing their job the exact same way. It's not because they don't get it. It's because none of it was ever about their work.

How It Works

We show up. We listen. We build. We show.

Think of it like office hours — except instead of advice, you walk away with working proof of what's possible.

01

We Set Up Inside Your Organization

A physical space — could be a conference room, a lounge, a literal coffee bar. The point is it feels approachable, not like a corporate training event. We announce it internally with a simple ask: think about the problems in your workflow, the features you wish you had, the things that slow you down. Then come talk to us.

02

Your People Bring Us the Real Problems

One by one, individuals and small teams sit down and tell us what's actually hard about their work. Not what their manager thinks is hard. Not what showed up on a survey. The real thing — the workaround they've been living with for years, the report that takes a week, the data that never connects.

This is the expertise you've been sitting on. We're just creating the space to surface it.

03

We Take It Away and Build — Fast

Using tools like Claude, Claude Code, and rapid prototyping, we take the problems we've heard and start building. Not slide decks. Working examples. Functional prototypes. Things people can see and touch that directly address what they told us.

Some of these come together in hours. That speed is part of the point.

04

We Come Back and Show Them

Same day or within the week, we reconnect with the people who brought us their problems — in person or over a call. We walk through what we built, get their honest reaction, and capture what it would mean for the business if this capability existed at scale.

This is where the real conversation starts. Not "what can AI do?" but "what could we do with this?"

People don't learn to use AI by sitting in a workshop. They learn when it's applied to the thing they've been trying to solve for the last six months — and suddenly, it takes an afternoon.
