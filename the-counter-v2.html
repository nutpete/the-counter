How the Counter works

We show up with your teams for a few days.

We send them a brief on what defines a good problem to bring to us, to help build and visualize where AI could actually change how they work.

Together, we fill out a Problem Blueprint

01 — We Listen

Your people bring problems in their work. We listen, build, and document.

This could be something that eats their week, a workaround they've been dealing with, or a gap between what they have and what they need. We sit down, talk through it, and write out a Problem Blueprint to lay out what the problem is, how they get around it now, and what could change if they were able to fix it.

02 — We Build

Working tools in hours, not months.

We build actual tools, not mockups or decks. An operations team says they can't track something? We build how that could work and bring it back. Finance says they're pulling data from three systems? We show how they could connect. Speed shows people what's possible when AI comes from their problem, not a generic case study or guides on 'how to prompt.'

03 — We Reveal

Teams see what's possible to define what's next.

At the counter itself or a follow-up, we give live walkthroughs of what's been built based on their problems. The conversation shifts from "what could AI do" to "when can we have this." We help setup a "Counter Log" (a way to see everything built throughout the engagement) so your team can keep building, sharing ideas with one another so the momentum around AI starts to become real.

People don't adopt AI because you told them it's important. They adopt it when they see it solve the thing they've been struggling with.
