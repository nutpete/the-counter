Selected Work

A few projects that show how I think.

01
Identity Patent UX Systems

Universal Wallet Infrastructure

Worked on the future of digital identity — how people share their identifying documents without giving away the data itself. The premise: tokenize credentials so you grant one-time access, not permanent storage. Your data never leaves your wallet.

I heavily authored a patent defining the system and formula behind the experience — specifically, a trust score that determines how much friction a user encounters when sharing information based on how verified the requesting entity is. The higher the trust, the smoother the experience.

The trust score framework defined the entire UX system — a single formula that governs how identity verification feels for millions of users.

02
Financial Services Product Design Consumer Research

Credit Card Choice & Customization

Partnered with one of the most recognized credit cards in the market to explore a fundamental question: if you could customize your card, what would you actually change? And what's the minimum meaningful choice — the thing that makes it feel like yours?

We mapped the full anatomy of the card, tested what mattered most to people, identified the floor of meaningful customization, and built a phased plan for rolling out choice features — grounded in what consumers actually valued, not what was easiest to ship.

Defined the minimum viable choice that makes customization feel meaningful — then built the rollout strategy to bring it to life.

03
Banking Operating Model Omnichannel

Branch Ecosystem Transformation

A comprehensive engagement to reimagine how a major bank's branch network operates — not just the customer experience, but the entire underlying system. Customer and colleague journeys. Org structure. Roles and skills. Coaching and incentive models. The full operating model, end to end.

The objective: evolve the branch ecosystem toward a true omnichannel experience that connects physical and digital — transforming not just how customers are served, but how employees work, how roles evolve, and how the organization is structured to deliver on that promise.

Defined the full breadth of what needs to change — across experience, people, talent, and operating model — to transform a physical branch network into something that actually works for the future.

04
AI Thought Leadership Capability Building

Defining How AI Changes Innovation

In early 2023, when generative AI was just starting to break through, I led the internal effort to define what it meant for how we work. Not a surface-level overview — a comprehensive framework for when to use AI, when not to, how it transforms the innovation process, and what changes for our clients.

That thinking became the foundation for everything that followed — it was used to sell multiple client engagements and boot camps, upskill teams internally and externally, and establish a point of view that the consultancy could stand behind in the market.

Authored the foundational AI perspective for an entire consultancy — years before the market caught up. That thinking drove deals, capability building, and a clear competitive position.

How I Work

Embedded, hands-on, and in it with you.

I don't believe in consulting from the outside. The best work happens when you're close to the people, close to the problem, and building alongside the team — not handing them a deck to execute.

01

Start With the Real Problem

Not the brief. Not the symptom. The actual challenge your organization is trying to solve — the one that matters enough to commit to.

02

Bring AI Into the Work, Not Around It

I introduce AI tools in the context of solving the problem — not as a separate training exercise. Proficiency comes from doing, not from prompting workshops.

03

Build With the Team, Not For Them

I work alongside your people. They bring the domain expertise; I bring the methods, the AI fluency, and the ability to move fast. Together, we figure it out.

04

Leave Capability, Not Dependency

The goal isn't a long-term retainer. It's that your team walks away with the judgment, confidence, and skills to keep going without me.

People don't learn AI from a boot camp. They learn it when it solves the problem they've been stuck on for six months.

That's what I build toward — the moment where the technology stops being abstract and starts being yours. Where the work changes, and the person doing it changes with it.
