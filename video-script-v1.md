## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Recording setup:**
- Talking head: natural light from a window (face it, don't sit with it behind you). Clean background — wall, bookshelf, whatever feels real. Not a ring light unless you want to.
- Audio: AirPods or any decent mic. Audio quality matters more than video quality.
- Screen share: Record with Loom (free, gives you face-in-corner automatically) or QuickTime screen recording + separate camera.

**Pacing tips:**
- Don't rush the screen share. Let people see the UI for a beat before you explain it.
- Click deliberately — not too fast. Each click should feel intentional.
- It's okay to pause. A half-second of silence while something loads or while you navigate feels natural.

**What to anonymize on JTech:**
- Company name (blur or replace with something generic)
- Customer names (blur or use test data)
- Any identifying details
- Keep the structure and complexity visible — that's the point

**Editing (if any):**
- Keep it mostly one take. A few jump cuts are fine and feel authentic.
- Speed up any loading or navigation that takes more than 2 seconds (2-4x speed).
- Don't add music. The voice-over-screen-share format works best clean.

**Where this lives on the site:**
- The full video could live in the proof section or as a standalone "See Our Work" page
- Consider also cutting the expense tracker portion (~40 sec) as a standalone clip for the "simple" proof card
- And the JTech walkthrough (~60 sec) as a standalone clip for the "deep build" proof card
- The hook + close (~40 sec) could work as the hero video if trimmed tight
